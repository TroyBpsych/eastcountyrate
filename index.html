The events that bent the lines

§4 · Five reasons the curves moved
2015 · The peak

Births topped out

San Diego County hit 44,442 births in 2015 — the high-water mark for the past decade. From here, the line bent down. By 2023 the county was at 35,713, a decline of 19.6%.

2019–2024 · The shift

Independent study took over

Pacific Coast Academy, a Dehesa-authorized independent-study charter, grew from 2,797 students to 6,760 — more than doubling — while in-person charters held flat or shrank. The structural charter story in East County is the IS model.

2021 · Pandemic conceptions

The sharpest drop

Births fell to 36,611 in 2021, down 6.3% in a single year. Babies conceived during the lockdown spring of 2020 became the smallest cohort of the decade — kindergarteners in 2026–27.

2023–2024 · The newcomers

CVUSD jumped 19%

Cajon Valley Unified added 2,920 students in one year, going from 15,607 to 18,527. Per the district's own LCAP: 2,300 new students enrolled Aug 2023–Apr 2024, with 39% arriving from another country. El Cajon's role as a receiving district reshapes enrollment in ways births can't predict.

2019–2026 · The leading indicator

LFCS applications fell 44%

Literacy First's kindergarten applications dropped from 318 in 2019 to 178 in 2026. That's more than twice the decline in county births over the same period — suggesting demand is shifting away from in-person charters faster than the demographic baseline.
